Overview

What Is This Service?

The "Establish a New Zone" service is the official digital application process for designating a new Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in Jamaica. It is administered through the Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority (SEZA) and coordinated with three additional government agencies.

Upon successful completion and approval, you receive a Developer License authorizing the establishment and operation of the proposed zone.

Who Can Apply?

Any corporate entity registered in Jamaica (or with a registered presence) that meets these criteria:

  • Incorporated and registered at the Companies Office of Jamaica (COJ)
  • Holds a valid Tax Registration Number (TRN) issued by TAJ
  • Has minimum issued and paid-up share capital of USD 1,500,000
  • Has an identified site for the proposed zone
  • Has a concept or final Master Plan for the zone

Agencies Involved

AgencyFull NameRole
SEZAJamaica Special Economic Zone AuthorityLead authority — evaluates, approves, and issues the Developer License
JCAJamaica Customs AgencyReviews customs aspects and issues a no-objection letter
TAJTax Administration JamaicaReviews tax compliance and issues a no-objection letter
MOFPSMinistry of Finance & Public ServicesReviews fiscal aspects and issues a no-objection letter
The entire process is handled online through eRegistrations Jamaica. You do not need to visit any government office in person to submit your application.

Prerequisites & What You'll Need

Before You Begin

Gather the following before starting your application. Having everything ready will save you significant time.

Company Information

  • Company name, type, and registration number at COJ
  • Tax Registration Number (TRN) — 9-digit number
  • Date of incorporation in Jamaica
  • Registered address, phone, and email
  • Authorized representative name, phone, and email
  • Shareholder details (names, nationalities, ownership percentages)
  • Proof of paid-up share capital of at least USD 1,500,000

Zone Information

  • Proposed name for the zone
  • Zone type: Multi-occupant or Single-occupant
  • Zone focus: Multi-purpose or Single-purpose
  • Parish and address where the zone will be located
  • Total land area (with unit of measurement)
  • Authorized activities to be carried out in the zone
  • Land parcel details (designation, ownership status, rights)

Documents (PDF format)

You will need at least 30+ documents. See the full checklist below. Key documents include:

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Articles of Incorporation
  • Tax Compliance Certificate
  • Proof of paid-up share capital
  • Concept and/or Final Master Plan
  • Certificate of Title (land rights)
  • Business Plan document
  • Compliance documents (H&S, Security, Disaster Recovery, Customs)

Technical Requirements

  • A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari)
  • Stable internet connection
  • An eRegistrations Jamaica account (register at jamaica.eregistrations.org)
All document uploads must be in PDF format. Scan physical documents to PDF before starting your application. Maximum file size per upload is typically 10 MB.

What Happens After Submission

Your application goes through a 7-phase, 21-step review process involving SEZA staff and three external agencies. Here is what happens:

Phase 1: Document Check

SEZA staff reviews your application for completeness. All 33 required documents are verified. If anything is missing, you will receive a correction request to upload the missing items.

Phase 2: Expert Evaluations (4 parallel reviews)

Your application is simultaneously evaluated by four specialized SEZA teams:

  • Legal Evaluation — legal aspects of the zone proposal
  • Technical Evaluation — infrastructure, site suitability, feasibility
  • Business Evaluation — business plan viability, economic impact, job creation
  • Compliance Evaluation — financial integrity, H&S, disaster recovery, security, customs

Phase 3: External Agency Consultations

Consultation documents are sent to three agencies. Each agency reviews your application and issues a "No objection" or "Objection" decision:

  • JCA — Jamaica Customs Agency
  • TAJ — Tax Administration Jamaica
  • MOFPS — Ministry of Finance & Public Services

Phase 4: Internal Approvals

Senior SEZA management signs off on each evaluation area (Legal, Technical, Business, Compliance).

Phase 5: Application Review Committee (ARC)

A committee reviews the consolidated evaluations, agency recommendations, risk assessments, and conditions. They may request additional information (corrections loop) or approve.

Phase 6: Status Letter

An official status/decision letter is drafted and signed by SEZA leadership.

Phase 7: Board & Final Approval

The complete application package is submitted to the SEZA Board. After CEO validation and Board review, the final decision is made. If approved, your Developer License is issued.

Corrections Requests

At certain stages (particularly after Document Check or ARC review), SEZA may request additional information or corrections from you. When this happens:

  1. You will see a notification on your dashboard indicating corrections are needed
  2. Log in and open your application
  3. Review the correction request details
  4. Make the requested changes or upload additional documents
  5. Click "Validate send page" to resubmit your corrections
  6. Your application re-enters the review process from where it was returned
The entire review process typically takes several weeks to months, depending on the complexity of your proposal and the responsiveness of all parties involved.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I save my application and come back later?
Yes. Your application is automatically saved as a draft. You can close the browser and return at any time. Just log in and find your draft application on the dashboard.
What file formats are accepted for uploads?
All uploads must be in PDF format. Maximum file size is typically 10 MB per document. If you have physical documents, scan them to PDF before uploading.
How long does the review process take?
The process involves 21 steps across 4 agencies. Typical processing time is several weeks to months, depending on the complexity of your proposal and the responsiveness of external agencies (JCA, TAJ, MOFPS).
What happens if my application is rejected?
If your application is rejected at any stage, you will see the updated status on your dashboard. The rejection reason will be documented. You may be able to submit a new application after addressing the issues identified.
What if SEZA requests corrections?
You will see a notification on your dashboard. Log in, open your application, review the requested corrections, make the changes or upload additional documents, and click "Validate send page" to resubmit.
The dropdown fields are empty when I click them. What do I do?
Dropdown fields in this form are search-based. You need to start typing at least 2-3 characters of what you're looking for (e.g., type "King" for Kingston). Wait 2-3 seconds for the results to appear, then click on the correct option.
I can't paste my TRN number. What's wrong?
The TRN field uses an input mask (___-___-___). You must type each digit individually. Copy-paste will not work correctly. Type your 9 digits slowly, one at a time.
The "Submit application" button is disabled. What do I do?
This means there are unfilled required fields. Go back through each tab (Project Overview, Developer, Master Plan, Business Plan, Compliance) and look for fields marked with a red asterisk (*) that are still empty. Also ensure you've completed the Payment tab and checked all consent boxes on the Send tab.
What is the minimum share capital required?
The minimum issued and paid-up share capital is USD 1,500,000. You must provide documentary proof of this amount (bank statement, auditor's letter, or similar).
Can I apply if my company is registered outside Jamaica?
You must have a registered presence in Jamaica to apply. If your company is foreign-owned, you typically need a locally registered subsidiary or joint venture. Answer "Yes" to the Joint Venture / Special Purpose Vehicle question and provide details of the parent company.
What is the "No objection" letter?
A no-objection letter is a formal statement from a government agency (JCA, TAJ, or MOFPS) confirming they have no objection to your zone proposal from their regulatory perspective. Your application requires no-objection from all three agencies to proceed.
How many land parcels can I include in my zone?
You can include multiple parcels. Add each parcel as a separate row in the Land Parcels grid on the Project Overview tab. Be sure to click "Save" after entering each row's details.
