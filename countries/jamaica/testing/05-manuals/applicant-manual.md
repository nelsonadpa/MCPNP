## 1. Overview

### What Is This Service?

The "Establish a new zone" service is the official digital application process for designating a new Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in Jamaica. It is administered through the Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority (SEZA) and coordinated with three additional government agencies. Upon successful completion and approval, the applicant receives a Developer License authorizing the establishment and operation of the proposed zone.

### Who Can Apply?

Any corporate entity registered in Jamaica (or with a registered presence in Jamaica) that meets the following criteria:

- Is incorporated and registered at the Companies Office of Jamaica (COJ)
- Holds a valid Tax Registration Number (TRN) issued by TAJ
- Has minimum issued and paid-up share capital of USD 1,500,000
- Has lawful access to the proposed site (through ownership, lease, or documented rights process)

### What You Need Before Starting

Before beginning the application, gather the following:

1. **Corporate documents** -- Certificate of incorporation, articles of incorporation, tax compliance certificate, proof of paid-up capital
2. **Site and land documents** -- Certificate(s) of title or lease agreements, surveyor's reports, site plans, affidavits of land ownership/no dispute
3. **Master plan materials** -- Concept and/or final master plan, land use maps, infrastructure layouts, design renderings, technical drawings
4. **Business plan** -- A complete business plan document covering investment projections, revenue model, job creation, and occupant pipeline
5. **Compliance plans** -- Occupational Health & Safety plan, Disaster Mitigation & Recovery plan, Security plan
6. **Financial documentation** -- Evidence of project funding source, due diligence documentation (if applicable)
7. **Proof of payment** -- Receipt or bank transfer confirmation for the application fee

> **Tip:** All document uploads must be in PDF format. Prepare all 33 documents before starting to avoid delays.

### Estimated Processing Time

The full review cycle involves seven phases (see Section 4). Total processing time depends on application completeness and agency review schedules. An incomplete application will be returned for corrections, adding to the overall timeline.

### Institutions Involved

| Institution | Full Name | Role |
|-------------|-----------|------|
| **SEZA** | Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority | Lead authority. Receives, evaluates, and approves the application. Issues the Developer License. |
| **JCA** | Jamaica Customs Agency | Reviews customs-related aspects and issues a No-Objection letter. |
| **MOFPS** | Ministry of Finance & Public Services | Reviews fiscal and financial aspects and issues a No-Objection letter. |
| **TAJ** | Tax Administration Jamaica | Reviews tax compliance and issues a No-Objection letter. |

## 2. Getting Started

### Step 1: Access the Platform

Open your web browser and navigate to:

**https://jamaica.eregistrations.org**

### Step 2: Create an Account or Log In

- **New users:** Click "Register" and complete the account creation form with your name, email address, and a secure password. You will receive a confirmation email.
- **Returning users:** Enter your email and password, then click "Log In."

### Step 3: Start a New Application

1. After logging in, you will see the **Dashboard**.
2. Locate the button labeled **"Establish a new zone"** and click it.
3. The system will create a new application and open the form editor.

### Step 4: Understand the Application Structure

The application is organized into three top-level tabs:

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **Form** | The main application form with five sub-tabs: Project Overview, Developer, Master Plan, Business Plan, and Compliance |
| **Payment** | Upload proof of payment for the application fee |
| **Send** | Review consent declarations, validate the form, and submit the application |

Within the **Form** tab, you will navigate between five sub-tabs using the horizontal navigation bar. The **Compliance** sub-tab further contains six side-navigation sections.

> **Tip:** The system auto-saves your progress when you click "Save." You can leave and return to a partially completed application at any time from the Dashboard.

### 3.1 Tab: Form > Project Overview

This tab captures the essential facts about the proposed zone: its name, type, location, and intended activities. There are approximately 14 fields organized across three sections.

Click the **"Form"** tab at the top, then ensure the **"Project overview"** sub-tab is selected.

| 3 | **Zone focus** | Radio | Yes | Select one: **Multi-purpose** (multiple activity types) or **Single-purpose** (one activity type only). |

#### Section: Site Location

| # | Field Label | Type | Required | What to Enter |
|---|-------------|------|----------|---------------|

### 3.7 Tab: Send

Click the **"Send"** tab at the top of the application.

This tab contains consent declarations and the submission controls.

#### Step 1: Review and Accept Consent Declarations

You will see one or more consent checkboxes. Read each declaration carefully and check the box to indicate your agreement. Typical declarations include:

- Acknowledgment that all information provided is true and accurate
- Consent for SEZA to verify the information with relevant agencies
- Agreement to comply with the Special Economic Zones Act and regulations

**You must check all consent boxes before the application can be submitted.**

#### Step 2: Validate the Form

1. Click the **"Form"** tab temporarily.
2. Click the **"Validate the form"** button located at the bottom of the form.
3. The system will check all required fields across all tabs.
4. If there are errors, a red error panel will appear listing the missing or invalid fields. Navigate to each field and correct the issue.
5. If validation passes with no errors, you are ready to submit.

> **Important:** Validation checks all tabs simultaneously. Even if the tab you are viewing looks complete, there may be required fields on other tabs. The error messages will indicate which fields need attention.

#### Step 3: Submit the Application

1. Return to the **"Send"** tab.
2. Ensure all consent checkboxes are checked (they may uncheck if you navigated away).
3. Click the **"Submit application"** button.
4. Wait for the submission to process (this may take 15-30 seconds due to the volume of uploaded documents).
5. Upon successful submission, you will see a confirmation message and/or be redirected to the Dashboard.

> **Important:** Once submitted, you cannot edit the application unless it is returned to you for corrections by a reviewing officer.

## 4. After Submission

### What Happens Next

Your application proceeds through seven phases of review:

| Phase | What Happens | Who Is Involved |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **1. Document Check** | A SEZA officer verifies that all 33 required documents are present, valid, and legible. | SEZA Intake Team |
| **2. Parallel Evaluations** | Four evaluation teams review your application simultaneously: Legal, Technical, Business, and Compliance. | SEZA Evaluation Teams |
| **3. External Consultations** | SEZA sends your application to three external agencies for No-Objection review. | JCA, TAJ, MOFPS |
| **4. Internal Approvals** | Senior SEZA management signs off on each evaluation domain. | SEZA Directors and COO |
| **5. Application Review Committee (ARC)** | The ARC reviews the consolidated evaluations, agency recommendations, and risk assessments. | SEZA ARC Committee |
| **6. Decision & Status Letter** | A formal status/decision letter is drafted and signed. | SEZA Management |
| **7. Board & Final Decision** | The application package is submitted to the SEZA Board for final approval. Upon approval, the Developer License and related documents are issued. | SEZA Board |

### Expected Status Changes

As your application moves through the workflow, its status will update on your Dashboard:

1. **Submitted** -- Application received, awaiting document check
2. **Under Review** -- Being evaluated by SEZA and external agencies
3. **Corrections Requested** -- One or more items need your attention (see below)
4. **Approved** -- Application approved by the Board
5. **License Issued** -- Developer License available for download

### How Corrections Work

If a reviewing officer identifies issues with your application:

1. The application status changes to **"Complementary Information"** (corrections requested).
2. You will receive a notification (check your Dashboard and registered email).
3. Log in to eRegistrations and open the application from your Dashboard.
4. The system will indicate which fields or documents need correction.
5. Make the requested changes and upload revised documents as needed.
6. Click **"Validate send page"** and resubmit the application.
7. The application returns to the review queue at the Document Check phase.

### Where to Find Your Certificate

Once your application is fully approved and the Developer License is issued:

1. Log in to **https://jamaica.eregistrations.org**
2. Go to your **Dashboard**
3. Open the completed application
4. Download the **Developer License** document from the application's document section

## 6. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **SEZA** | Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority -- the lead regulatory body that oversees SEZ applications, evaluations, and licensing. |
| **JCA** | Jamaica Customs Agency -- the government agency responsible for customs administration. Issues a No-Objection letter as part of the SEZ application process. |
| **MOFPS** | Ministry of Finance & Public Services -- the government ministry responsible for fiscal policy. Issues a No-Objection letter as part of the SEZ application process. |
| **TAJ** | Tax Administration Jamaica -- the government agency responsible for tax collection and compliance. Issues a No-Objection letter as part of the SEZ application process. |
| **SEZ** | Special Economic Zone -- a geographically delimited area within Jamaica where special economic rules, tax incentives, and regulatory frameworks apply to promote investment and economic activity. |
| **ARC** | Application Review Committee -- an internal SEZA committee that reviews the consolidated evaluations, agency recommendations, and risk assessments before making a recommendation to the Board. |
| **NOC** | No-Objection Certificate/Letter -- a document issued by an external agency (JCA, TAJ, or MOFPS) indicating that they have reviewed the application and have no objections to the zone designation. |
| **COJ** | Companies Office of Jamaica -- the government registry for company incorporation and registration. |
| **TRN** | Tax Registration Number -- a unique 9-digit identification number issued by TAJ to all taxpayers in Jamaica. |
| **NWC** | National Water Commission -- Jamaica's primary water utility provider. |
| **OHS / OSH** | Occupational Health and Safety / Occupational Safety and Health -- regulatory standards governing workplace safety. ISO 45001 is the international standard. |
| **EditGrid** | An interactive table within the form where you can add, edit, and save multiple rows of data (e.g., shareholders, land parcels, project phases). |
| **Choices.js dropdown** | A search-based dropdown field. You must type at least a few characters to trigger an API search. Wait for results to load, then click to select. |
| **Developer** | In the context of this application, the company or entity proposing to establish and operate the Special Economic Zone. The Developer receives the Developer License upon approval. |
| **Greenfield** | A site that has not been previously developed or built upon. |
| **Brownfield** | A site that has been previously developed and may contain existing structures or infrastructure. |
| **Developer License** | The official license document issued by SEZA upon successful completion of the application process, authorizing the establishment and operation of the Special Economic Zone. |
