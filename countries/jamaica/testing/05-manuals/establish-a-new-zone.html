1 Introduction

This manual guides you through the online application process for establishing a Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in Jamaica. The service is operated by the Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority (JSEZA) through the eRegistrations platform.

What is this service?

If your company wants to develop a designated area in Jamaica as a Special Economic Zone — offering manufacturing, logistics, BPO, or other economic activities under special regulatory and fiscal conditions — this is the application you need to file. Upon approval, you will receive a Developer License authorizing you to build and operate the zone.

What will you receive?

A successful application produces four official outputs:

  1. Approval from JSEZA — The primary authorization for your zone.
  2. No-Objection Letter from JCA (Jamaica Customs Agency) — Confirming customs compliance.
  3. No-Objection Letter from MOFPS (Ministry of Finance & Public Services) — Confirming fiscal/financial viability.
  4. No-Objection Letter from TAJ (Tax Administration Jamaica) — Confirming tax compliance.

Who are the institutions involved?

InstitutionFull NameRole
JSEZAJamaica Special Economic Zone AuthorityLead authority. Receives, evaluates, and approves applications. Issues the Developer License.
JCAJamaica Customs AgencyReviews customs aspects and issues a No-Objection letter.
MOFPSMinistry of Finance & Public ServicesReviews fiscal and financial aspects and issues a No-Objection letter.
TAJTax Administration JamaicaReviews tax compliance and issues a No-Objection letter.

How long does it take?

Processing time varies depending on the completeness of your application and the complexity of your proposal. Once submitted, your application passes through 7 phases of review (detailed in the "After Submission" section). A complete, well-prepared application is processed significantly faster than one that requires corrections.

Tip: Prepare everything before starting
The online form is extensive (7 tabs, over 40 document uploads). You can save your progress and return later, but gathering all your documents first will make the process much smoother.

2 Prerequisites — What to Gather Before You Start

Before you begin filling out the online form, make sure your company meets the eligibility criteria and that you have gathered the necessary documents.

Eligibility Requirements

To apply, your company must:

  • Be a corporate entity registered in Jamaica (or with a registered presence in Jamaica).
  • Be incorporated at the Companies Office of Jamaica (COJ) with a valid registration number.
  • Hold a valid Tax Registration Number (TRN) from Tax Administration Jamaica.
  • Have a minimum issued and paid-up share capital of USD 1,500,000. The system will show a warning if you enter a lower amount.
  • Have lawful access to the proposed site — either through ownership, a long-term lease, or a documented process to acquire rights.
Warning: Capital threshold
If your company's paid-up capital is below USD 1,500,000, you can still fill out the form, but a warning message will appear. Be prepared to address this if your application is questioned.

3 Getting Started — Account & Login

Creating an Account

  1. Go to https://jamaica.eregistrations.org in your web browser.
  2. Click "Create an account" on the login page.
  3. Enter your name, email address, and choose a password.
  4. Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link.
  5. Return to the login page and sign in with your credentials.

Starting a New Application

  1. After logging in, you will see the list of available services.
  2. Find "Establish a new zone" and click on it.
  3. A new application will be created. You will see the form with 7 tabs across the top.
  4. Work through each tab from left to right. You can navigate between tabs at any time, and your progress is saved automatically.
Note: Save as you go
The system automatically saves your entries as you fill them in. You can close your browser and return later to continue where you left off. Your draft application will be waiting in your dashboard.
Warning: One application at a time
You cannot submit multiple applications for the same zone simultaneously. If you need to start over, contact JSEZA for guidance.

Validate Submit

The Submit button becomes active only after validation passes and all consent boxes are checked.

How to Submit

  1. Check all three consent boxes. All three are required. Read each one carefully before checking.
  2. Click "Validate". The system will check all required fields across all tabs. If anything is missing or invalid, you will see a list of errors with links to the specific fields that need attention.
  3. Fix any errors. Navigate to the tabs mentioned in the error list, correct the issues, then return to the Send tab and validate again.
  4. Click "Submit". This button only becomes active after validation passes successfully and all consent boxes are checked.
  5. Confirmation. After submission, you will see a confirmation message and your application moves to the review queue.
Important: Submission is final
Once you click Submit, you cannot modify your application unless JSEZA sends it back to you for corrections (the "Complementary Information" step in the workflow). Double-check everything before submitting.
Tip: Common validation errors
  • Missing required fields (marked with *) in any tab
  • Required document uploads that were skipped
  • Consent checkboxes not checked
  • Conditional fields that appeared (e.g., after selecting "Yes") but were left empty
  • Invalid email addresses or phone numbers

11 After Submission — What Happens Next

Once you submit your application, it enters a multi-phase review process involving 21 active roles across 7 phases. Here is what to expect:

1
Phase 1: Initial Review
Documents Check — A case file specialist at JSEZA reviews your application for completeness. If documents are missing or unclear, they may send your application back with a request for additional information.
2
Phase 2: Evaluation (runs in parallel)
Five separate evaluations happen simultaneously:
  • Legal Evaluation (Legal Services Unit) — Reviews corporate documents, land rights, compliance with the SEZ Act
  • Technical Evaluation (Technical Services & Inspection) — Reviews master plan, infrastructure, design
  • Business Evaluation (Business Planning & Strategy) — Reviews business plan, financial viability
  • Compliance Evaluation (Compliance & Assessment) — Reviews OH&S, security, disaster plans
  • Organize NOC and Inspection — Coordinates the agency review process
3
Phase 3: Agency Approvals (runs in parallel)
Three government agencies review your application simultaneously:
  • JCA (Jamaica Customs Agency) — Customs and border readiness
  • TAJ (Tax Administration Jamaica) — Tax compliance verification
  • MOFPS (Ministry of Finance) — Fiscal/financial assessment
Each agency issues a No-Objection letter if satisfied.
4
Phase 4: Internal Approvals (runs in parallel)
Four internal JSEZA approvals based on the evaluations from Phase 2:
  • Business Approval
  • Technical Approval
  • Compliance Approval
  • Legal Approval
5
Phase 5: Committee & Management
  • ARC (Application Review Committee) — Consolidates all evaluations and agency responses
  • Complementary Information — If the ARC needs more details, a request is sent back to you. You will be notified by email and must respond through the platform.
  • Status Letter — A formal letter is prepared summarizing the review outcome
  • Signature — The Status Letter is signed by authorized JSEZA management
6
Phase 6: Board Decision
  • Board Submission — The complete application package is prepared for the Board
  • CEO Validation — The CEO of JSEZA validates the submission
  • Board — The JSEZA Board makes the final decision: "Approved" or "Denied"
7
Phase 7: Post-Decision
  • If Approved: SEZ Documents are prepared and your Developer License is issued. You will receive the license through the platform.
  • If Denied: A formal Denial Letter is issued explaining the reasons.
Note: Track your application
You can log into the eRegistrations platform at any time to check the current status of your application. The system shows which phase your application is in and any pending actions on your part.
Warning: Respond promptly to requests
If JSEZA or any reviewing agency requests additional information (the "Complementary Information" step), respond as quickly as possible. Delays in providing requested information will delay your entire application.

13 Frequently Asked Questions

Can I save my application and come back later?

Yes. The system automatically saves your entries as you fill them in. You can close your browser, log out, and return at any time. Your draft application will be in your dashboard when you log back in.

What happens if I submit an incomplete application?

The validation step (on the Send tab) is designed to catch missing required fields. However, if you somehow submit with incomplete information, the case file specialist in Phase 1 (Documents Check) will review your application and may send it back to you with a request to provide the missing items. This will delay the process.

How do I know which documents are required for my specific situation?

The form uses conditional logic. For example, if you select that your land rights status is "In process of acquiring rights", an upload field for the supporting document will appear automatically. As a general rule: if an upload field is visible and marked with an asterisk (*), it is required.

Can I apply as a foreign company?

Yes, but your company must have a registered presence in Jamaica. If you are an external (foreign) company, select "External company" in the Company type dropdown. You will still need a Jamaica COJ registration number and a TRN. If you have not yet registered in Jamaica, you must do so through the Companies Office of Jamaica before applying.

What file formats can I upload?

PDF is strongly recommended for all documents. For site photos, JPEG and PNG are also accepted. The system may accept other common formats, but PDF ensures the best compatibility and readability for reviewers.

What if my paid-up capital is below USD 1,500,000?

You can still fill out and submit the form. The system will display a warning, but it will not block your submission. However, the minimum capital requirement is set by the SEZ Act, so your application may face challenges during evaluation. Contact JSEZA for guidance if this applies to your situation.

How long does the entire process take from submission to Developer License?

There is no fixed timeline. It depends on the completeness of your application, the complexity of the project, and the time required for agency reviews (JCA, TAJ, MOFPS). A complete, well-prepared application with no requests for additional information can be processed significantly faster. Monitor your dashboard regularly and respond promptly to any requests.

Can I modify my application after submission?

No, you cannot modify it directly after submission. The only way to update your application is if JSEZA sends it back to you through the "Complementary Information" step (Phase 5). In that case, you can make the requested changes and resubmit. If you realize there is an error, contact JSEZA immediately.

What is the application fee, and how do I pay it?

Contact JSEZA directly for the current fee schedule and payment instructions. Payment is typically made via bank transfer. You will upload proof of payment in Tab 6 (Payment) of the application form.

What if I need help filling out the form?

JSEZA offers support for applicants. Contact them through their website or by phone. You can also engage a local attorney or consultant experienced with SEZ applications to help you prepare and submit your application.

What does "No-Objection letter" mean?

A No-Objection letter means that the agency (JCA, TAJ, or MOFPS) has reviewed your application and has no objection to the establishment of the zone from their perspective. It is essentially a green light from that agency. All three No-Objection letters are needed for full approval.

Can I apply for multiple zones at the same time?

Each zone requires a separate application. If you plan to develop multiple zones, you must submit one complete application per zone, each with its own set of documents, master plan, and business plan.

14 Glossary of Terms

ARC (Application Review Committee)
A JSEZA internal committee that consolidates all evaluations and agency responses before the Board decision.
BPO (Business Process Outsourcing)
The practice of contracting business operations to third-party providers, often in customer service, IT support, or data processing.
COJ (Companies Office of Jamaica)
The government office responsible for company registration and corporate records in Jamaica.
Developer License
The official authorization issued by JSEZA that permits a company to develop and operate a Special Economic Zone.
EditGrid
A repeatable table in the form where you can add multiple rows (e.g., for shareholders, land parcels, or JV companies). Click "+ Add another" to create a new row.
JCA (Jamaica Customs Agency)
The government agency responsible for customs operations, border protection, and trade facilitation.
JSEZA (Jamaica Special Economic Zone Authority)
The government authority that regulates, promotes, and administers Special Economic Zones in Jamaica under the SEZ Act of 2016.
MOFPS (Ministry of Finance & Public Services)
The government ministry responsible for fiscal policy, public financial management, and economic policy.
No-Objection Letter
A formal letter from a government agency stating that it has no objection to the proposed zone from its area of responsibility.
NWC (National Water Commission)
Jamaica's public water utility, responsible for water supply and wastewater services across the island.
ODPEM (Office of Disaster Preparedness and Emergency Management)
The government agency responsible for disaster preparedness, response, and recovery in Jamaica.
OH&S / OSH
Occupational Health and Safety / Occupational Safety and Health. The discipline of ensuring safe working conditions.
SEZ (Special Economic Zone)
A designated geographic area with special regulatory and fiscal arrangements designed to attract investment and promote economic activity, as defined by Jamaica's Special Economic Zones Act (2016).
SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)
A legal entity created for a specific purpose, often to isolate financial risk. Joint ventures for zone development often use an SPV structure.
TAJ (Tax Administration Jamaica)
The government agency responsible for tax collection, compliance, and administration in Jamaica.
TRN (Tax Registration Number)
A unique identification number assigned by TAJ to every taxpayer (individual or corporate) in Jamaica. Required for all official transactions.
